Generative AI FAQs

Generative AI (GenAI) refers to artificial intelligence systems that can create new content, such as text, images, music, or code, based on patterns learned from existing data. Unlike traditional AI that analyzes data, GenAI generates original outputs. Examples include ChatGPT for text and DALL-E for images.

GenAI uses machine learning models, often based on neural networks like transformers, trained on large datasets. It predicts and generates new content by understanding patterns in the data. For instance, a language model like GPT learns from billions of text examples to produce coherent responses.

Popular tools include: ChatGPT (text generation), Midjourney (image creation), GitHub Copilot (code assistance), and Stable Diffusion (art and design). These are used in creative, educational, and professional contexts.

Strengths include rapid content creation, automation of repetitive tasks, enhancing creativity (e.g., brainstorming ideas), and accessibility for non-experts. In education, it can generate study aids; in daily life, it assists with writing or design.

Limitations include potential for inaccuracies, bias from training data, over-reliance leading to skill loss, and ethical concerns like misinformation or job displacement. Risks involve privacy breaches and misuse for harmful content.

While many tools have age restrictions, supervised use can be educational. However, risks like exposure to biased or inappropriate content exist. Always verify outputs and use parental controls.

Start by exploring free tools like ChatGPT or DALL-E. Learn basics through online courses on platforms like Coursera or edX. Experiment responsibly, and always consider ethical implications.

Ethical Use of AI

Responsible Use in Academe

Use AI as a tool for learning, not cheating. Cite AI-generated content and ensure human oversight in research.

Avoiding Plagiarism and Misuse

Always attribute AI outputs. Avoid using it to fabricate data or bypass effort in assignments.

Accuracy and Verification

Cross-check AI outputs with reliable sources. AI can hallucinate facts, so human verification is key.

Bias and Fairness

Be aware of biases in training data. Promote diverse datasets and ethical AI development.

Recommended Guidelines

  • Educate yourself on AI capabilities and limitations.
  • Use AI transparently in collaborative settings.
  • Prioritize privacy and data security.
  • Encourage ethical AI policies in schools and workplaces.
  • Support regulations for responsible AI deployment.

Analogy: AI in Dev Work

The Wedding Documentation Analogy

Scenario: You're organizing your wedding. The documentation team asks for 40k for the package. They'll use an iPhone 16 Pro Max to cover the event and CapCut for same-day edited videos. The team has 2 years of experience and an above-average portfolio.

Would I agree to the deal? No, I would not agree. 40k seems excessively high for a package relying on consumer-grade tools like an iPhone (which costs ~$1,200) and free software like CapCut. With only 2 years of experience, the pricing doesn't align with the value provided, especially compared to professional videographers using high-end equipment.

Justification of their work's integrity: The team's 2 years of experience and above-average portfolio suggest reliability and quality in delivery. They likely have a track record of capturing events well, ensuring timely edits, and maintaining professionalism. Integrity here means delivering on promises without cutting corners, backed by their portfolio as evidence.

Reflection on "vibe coders": "Vibe coders" might refer to AI-assisted developers or tools that generate code quickly for a trendy, aesthetic "vibe" (e.g., GitHub Copilot or similar). In dev work, they offer speed and creativity for prototyping but often lack deep skill, originality, or handling complex logic. Like the wedding team using basic tools for a premium price, "vibe coders" can produce functional code fast but may compromise on robustness, security, or innovation without human expertise. True integrity in dev requires balancing AI efficiency with skilled oversight to avoid bugs or ethical issues.
